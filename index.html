World Cup 2026 Takeover Pool Rules

Match Winner

The team that advances controls the bracket spot in the next round. ESPN final results are used to update the advancing team.

Full-Time Spread Grading

All spreads are graded using the score after regulation/full time only: 90 minutes plus stoppage time. Extra time and penalty kicks decide who advances, but they do not change who covered the spread.

Underdog Overtime Rule

If the underdog takes the favorite to extra time or penalties, the underdog covered. If the favorite later advances, the underdog owner takes over the favorite.

Example: Belgium -0.5 vs Senegal is tied after full time. Senegal covered. Belgium advances in extra time, so Dolan takes over Belgium from J. Schwartz.

Point Spread

The locked spread applies to the team listed on the left side of each matchup.

  • -0.5: favorite must be winning at full time to cover.
  • -1.5: favorite must be winning by 2+ at full time to cover.
  • -2.5: favorite must be winning by 3+ at full time to cover.
  • +0.5: underdog covers if tied or winning at full time.
  • PK / 0: no spread takeover; advancing team keeps its owner.

Takeover Rule

If the favorite advances but does not cover the locked full-time spread, the opposing owner takes control of the advancing team.

Example: England -1.5 beats DR Congo 2-1. England advances but does not cover, so McCartney takes over England from Joe.

No Takeover Example

Mexico -0.5 beats Ecuador 2-0 at full time. Mexico advances and covers, so Merc keeps Mexico.

Dashboard, Bracket, Owners

The dashboard shows ESPN refresh time, scores, takeovers, alive/eliminated teams, previous owner to new owner, and championship probability movement. The bracket carries owners forward based on takeover results.

Championship Probability

Monte Carlo odds recalculate after refresh/run using current ownership, completed takeovers, remaining bracket paths, locked spreads, full-time underdog cover overrides, and team strength ratings.
